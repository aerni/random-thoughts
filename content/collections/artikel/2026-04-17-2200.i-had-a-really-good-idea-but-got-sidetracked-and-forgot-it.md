A tragic story with no witnesses and no recovery plan. All of these things are better with donuts.

## The Vanishing Thought

Great ideas are fragile. They appear briefly, demand nothing, and disappear forever the moment you try to multitask. You assume they’ll come back. They never do.

## Distraction Mechanics

One notification. One quick check. One unrelated thought. Suddenly the idea is gone, replaced by something significantly less useful. The brain is not built for this level of chaos.

## Sugary Anchoring
A donut creates a pause. A moment. A physical anchor in time where thoughts are allowed to exist uninterrupted. It’s not a perfect system, but it’s better than trusting your memory.
