Sure, it looks harmless. Sleek. Helpful. Always there when you need it. But so are most things that slowly ruin your attention span. All of these things are better with donuts.

## Pocket-Sized Chaos

Your phone promises efficiency and delivers distraction with frightening precision. You unlock it to check one thing and emerge 27 minutes later knowing a stranger’s entire vacation itinerary. Meanwhile, your actual life waits patiently in another tab you forgot to open. A donut, however, has a single purpose. It does not lure you into side quests. It respects your time.

## Notification Anxiety

The tiny red dot is a master manipulator. It suggests urgency where none exists. It whispers: “This could be important.” It rarely is. A donut never interrupts you mid-thought. It does not buzz. It does not demand. It simply exists, quietly improving your situation without requiring a password.

## Analog Recovery

There is something deeply healing about engaging with an object that cannot connect to Wi-Fi. A donut cannot update, sync, or crash. It is fully present. In a world of constant digital noise, this counts as luxury.
