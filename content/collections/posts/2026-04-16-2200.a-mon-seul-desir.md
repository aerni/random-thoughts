The sixth has puzzled art historians for centuries. The prevailing theory is that it depicts the renunciation of desire itself; the lady setting aside earthly pleasures with the quiet dignity of someone who has simply had enough of them.'

        text: "What makes medieval imagery so endlessly alive is exactly this — the deadpan coexistence of the sacred and the strange. Rabbits nibble flowers at the lady's feet. A tiny dog sits on the table like a paperweight. Birds perch in impossible orange trees. Everything is symbolic, layered, and also somehow deeply domestic. A lion holds open a tent flap with the weary competence of a hotel doorman."
